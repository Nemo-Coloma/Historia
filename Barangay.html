Asinan

Originally a salt-making area, its name is derived from the Filipino word "asin," meaning salt. It has since evolved into a densely populated residential area with vibrant local markets.

Banicain

Its strategic location near Subic Bay made it an essential area for maritime activities. Over time, it has developed a mix of residential zones and commercial enterprises, reflecting its rich maritime culture.

Barretto

Named after a prominent family, the Barretto family, this barangay developed into a popular tourist destination, renowned for its beautiful beaches and nightlife.

East Bajac-Bjac

Historically a trading center, East Bajac-Bajac grew as a commercial hub, providing essential services and goods to the local population.

East Tapinac

Known for its educational institutions, East Tapinac has always been a center for learning and community activities, contributing to the city’s intellectual and cultural development.

Gordon Heights

Named after James Leonard Gordon, a significant figure in Olongapo’s history, it was developed to provide housing for the city’s growing population, offering scenic views and a peaceful environment.

Kalaklan

Known for its historical significance and strategic location near the Kalaklan River, this barangay has always been vital for trade and transportation.

Mabayuan

This area has a long history of being a residential and agricultural zone, fostering a close-knit community with various local businesses.

New Cabalan

Developed as a suburban area to accommodate the growing population, it blends residential living with agricultural activities.

New Ilalim

Centrally located, it developed to offer a mix of residential spaces and small businesses, catering to the diverse needs of the city’s residents.

New Kababae

Initially developed to house workers of the U.S. Naval Base, it has grown into a mixed residential and commercial area, known for its diverse community and proximity to Subic Bay.

New Kalalake

Established to accommodate the influx of workers and their families during the construction of the U.S. Naval Base, it has grown into a densely populated residential area with a variety of local businesses and services.

Old Cabalan

One of the older settlements, it features a mix of traditional homes and new developments, maintaining a strong community spirit.

Pag-asa

Established to provide hope and new beginnings for residents, "Pag-Asa" means hope in Filipino. It developed into a diverse urban area with commercial and residential sections.

Sta Rita

Named after Saint Rita, this barangay is known for its active local markets and strong community life, reflecting its deep-rooted religious and cultural traditions.

West Bajac-Bjac

Historically significant as a dense residential and commercial area, it continues to be a bustling part of the city with numerous shops and schools.

West Tapinac

Known for its educational institutions, East Tapinac has always been a center for learning and community activities, contributing to the city’s intellectual and cultural development.
